Hiking in Alaska

Peaks I've bagged

Pioneer Peak, Elevation: 6398 ft
Pionner Peak is a massive mountain that towers over Palmer. It gains over 6000 feet from the trailhead. I left on a Friday after work, camped, and summitted Saturday. Fun fact: Pioneer Peak is the mountain on the Alaska driver's license.







O'Malley Peak, Elevation: 5184 ft
O'Malley Peak is a sharp, jagged looking peak that dominates the skylike of the Anchorage Bowl. It is the highest peak readily visible from South Anchorage. This one is easily doable in a day. A friend and I decided it was the most patriotic way we could possibly spend the Fourth of July in Anchorage. We went up Ballfield and the Northwest Gully and came down the West Ridge, which took a little longer, but was totally worth it.





Wolverine Peak, Elevation: 4491 ft
Wolverine Peak is on the western edge of the Chugach State Park's mountains. It's about ten miles round trip from Prospect Heights trailhead and can be climbed year round.








Rendezvous Peak, Elevation: 4068 ft
Rendezvous Peak is in the Arctic Valley Ski Area in north Anchorage. It's a great hike for beginners and you can make it to the top in just a couple hours.








Other trips

Crow Pass
Crow Pass is a beautiful 21 mile trail that connects Girdwood and Eagle River. It takes a couple days to do and includes a very cold river crossing!








Bold Peak Attempt
A friend and I attemtped Bold Peak in June, but came up short of the summit due to wasting several hours finding the right gully to go up. We're both itching to go back and bag this one for real!








Williwaw Lakes
Williwaw Lakes is a group of nine alpine lakes about six miles from the Glen Alps Trailhead. It's doable as a long day hike or mellow backpacking trip. We hiked in on a Saturday afternoon and camped by the last lake with a great view of Mount Williwaw. I'd like to go back next summer and bag Mount Williwaw while I'm out there.
